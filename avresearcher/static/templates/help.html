Help

AVResearcherXL is a comparative interface, consisting of two identical search boxes, in red and in blue. Result sets obtained with each side of the interface can be compared by means of word clouds, histograms, and a timeline. The language of the majority of data is Dutch.

Collections

The tool contains two collections:

The catalogue iMMix of the Netherlands Institute for Sound and Vision: the television and radio catalogue of public broadcasters from 1900 until November 2013. The iMMix collection exists of programme descriptions. From 2010 onwards, some tv programme descriptions are enriched with subtitles and tweets about the tv programmes. Moreover, for a few radio broadcasts, we also generated speech recognition files (early February 2012 until January 2014).

A selection of the catalogue of Dutch newspaper articles of KB Royal Archive of the Netherlands: De Telegraaf (1900-1994), Leeuwarder Courant (1900-1995), De Tijd-De Maasbode (1959-1969), De Waarheid (1940-1990), Nieuwsblad van Friesland (1901-1994) and Limburger Koerier (1920-1975).

Search

In the search box you can enter your query, i.e. any word or word combination (in Dutch). The interface will retrieve all documents that contain this word or word combination. The default setting for word combinations is AND. For instance, when typing Poolse migrant, the interface searches for every document that contains both words. See table at the right for an overview of all possible search strings (OR, NOT, etc). Stopwords such as ‘de’, ‘een’ etc are removed from the index.

Your search can be specified by indicating in which field () of the document you want to search. For iMMix, the fields are:

  • iMMix program titles: title of radio or television programme. Often, they have more than one title (title of programme, title of episode etc). In that case, the interface searches in both titles.
  • iMMix program descriptions: extensive description provided by professional annotators
  • iMMix T888: subtitles for the hearing impaired, sparsely from 1982 onwards, more from 2006 onwards and structural from 13 november 2012 onwards
  • iMMix tweets: collection of tweets about Dutch television programmes, 2012 until 2013. Tweets are collected on the basis of a ‘media list’: hashtags of television programmes and channels such as #DWDD or #NOS

For KB, the fields are:

  • title: title of the article
  • text: text of article

To clear all results, use the cross () in the right corner of the search box.

Time slider

Below the search box, there is a time slider. The time slider displays the range of broadcasting date, resp. publication date of all documents that match your query. The left date indicates the first document; the right date the most recent one. The time sliders can be moved/narrowed down.

Word clouds

AVResearcherXL provides visualizations to support exploration of your query. It contains: word clouds, barcharts and a timeline. The word clouds, barcharts and the timeline are calculated by means of so-called ‘facets’. A facet is a characteristic of a certain collection. Each collection has different facets.

The facets of iMMix are:

  1. word: most descriptive terms generated by algorithms
  2. tag: keywords provided by the annotators of the iMMix catalogue
  3. channel: broadcasters
  4. people: persons provided by the annotators of the iMMix catalogue
  5. genre: genres provided by the annotators of the iMMix catalogue

The facets of KB are:

  1. word: most descriptive terms generated by algorithms
  2. source: title of the newspaper
  3. type: article, advertisement, family announcement, illustration

The ‘most descriptive terms’ are generated as follows: all punctuation marks are removed, all capital letters are removed (which means that differences in meaning between words with capital and without capital are also erased). It displays the most unique words for each document. This method implies that words which contain spelling-mistakes (such as OCR-mistakes in the newspaper scans or speech-recognition mistakes in the subtitles) will often be considered as unique for the document and, hence, might appear in the word cloud.

The word cloud also functions as filter for your query. Once you select a term in the wordcloud, the term will be added to your query. For example if you select NOS in the word cloud of the facet ‘channel’, the interface shows only the radio and television broadcasts of the channel NOS. Excluding a term from the word cloud can be done by means of boolean operators in the search box. For instance when typing Pools -voetbal -wielrennen, the interface will search for documents that contain the word Pools, but exclude the words voetbal and wielrennen.

Bar charts

The bar charts contain the same information as the word clouds, but in bars. They rank the highest frequencies per hit, from high to low numbers, hence enabling to compare the left and right query in a more structured way. In contrast to the word clouds, words in the bar charts are not clickable, neither function as filters.

Time line

The timeline visualizes the amount of documents which contains your query. The blue line visualizes the frequency of the left query, and the red line this of the right query. “1” means one document (=one television programme or one newspaper article). In order to understand how the time line is generated, mouse-overs indicate what the numbers mean.

When clicking on a ‘dot’, you can view the result list of the selected year/month/week/day in a pop-up window.

There is no equal distribution of television, radio broadcasts and newspapers over time. In the 1950s, 1960s and 1970s, a lot of television and radio programme information is missing, too. Since the number of television programmes and newspaper varies over time, we implemented a normalization option for the timeline (). These relative frequencies (percentages) are calculated by dividing the number of documents with hits by the total number of documents within one year (or week, day, depending of the ‘bucket’). A ‘document’ is a single newspaper article or tv/radio broadcast. The normalization helps to compare result lists of different collections and different selected fields.

Results list

The result lists show all documents that match your query. To paginate the documents, you can click on the page numbers at the bottom of the webpage.

A first sorting option is relevancy (). Relevancy is calculated according to standard information retrieval techniques and algorithms. An other sort option for the result list is date, ascending () or descending ().

For each document, snippets of the selected fields are displayed for the iMMix-collection. For the KB-collection, we do not show any snippets as newspaper texts are protected by copyrights of the journalist(s) who wrote the article. The documents open within the respective search systems of Sound and Vision and KB’s Delpher, which contain all relevant information and -for the KB- also a scan of the original newspaper article. The website in.beeldengeluid.nl does not contain the full text subtitles due to copyright restrictions.

Future developments

AVResearcherXL is a prototype and is continuously under development. If you have any suggestions for improvement or feedback, please contact Eva Baaren (ebaaren@beeldengeluid.nl).

We have already some ideas for extensions, such as:

  • integration with an overarching user management system, enabling to track search history, bookmark, export and save results
  • additional and removable search boxes
  • adding more collections, e.g. radio broadcasts
  • icons instead of text for fields
  • link to portal Academia.nl, providing access to streamed video material
  • more precise facets for iMMix (e.g. radio is now determined by a mention of #radio in the title; iMMix contains movies, which are not filterable with the current interface)
  • automatic updates of data through ‘live’ connection with iMMix and KB
  • highlight queries in iMMix-catalogue and KB
  • add more facets KB collection: persons, events etc
  • add more subtitles

oorlog vrede Containing both "oorlog" and "vrede". AND is the default operator.
oorlog OR vrede Containing "oorlog" or "vrede".
oorlog +vrede That must contain "vrede", and may contain oorlog.
oorlog -vrede Containing "oorlog", but not "vrede"
(oorlog OR vrede) AND iran Grouping is supported as well. Finds documents that contain either "oorlog" or "vrede", in combination with "iran".
"oorlog en vrede" Containing the exact phrase "oorlog en vrede".
"oorlog vrede"~3 Search for the terms "oorlog" and "vrede" occuring within 3 words of eachother in a document.
oorlog^4 vrede The relevance of a document is determined based on terms found in that document. Using the "^"-symbol enables the user to boost the importance of a term with a factor, and thereby influence the scoring algorithm. In this example, "oorlog" is considered 4 times as important as "vrede"
"vast en zeker"^4 "zeker en vast" Phrase queries can be boosted as well. The phrase "vast en zeker" is considered more important in scoring than "zeker en vast".
te?t Perform a single character wildcard search. Matches on terms with the single charachter replaced, such as "test" or "tent". Prefix wildcards are supported.
oorl* Performs a multiple character wildcard search. Finds documents containing terms with the prefix "oorl". Matches on "oorlog", but also on "oorlogsjaar".
*rlog Performs a multiple character wildcard search. Finds documents containing terms with the suffix "rlog". Matches on "oorlog", but also on "grondoorlog".
maximum~ Performs a fuzzy search using the Levenshtein (edit distance) algorithm. Matches up to 50 terms, ranked on how many characters in the query have to be replaced. Matches "maximum", and "Máxima".
maximum~0.8 The "fuzziness" of a fuzzy query can be controlled by an optional parameter. The parameter has a value between 0 and 1 with a value closer to 1 only terms with a higher similarity will be matched. The default value is 0.5.
